Noahic Covenant Awareness Month


Happy Noahic Covenant Awareness Month! In recent years, the celebration of Pride Month and the promotion of homosexuality have become increasingly prevalent in society. As such, the rainbow has been hijacked and subverted to promote a satanic and secularism ideology. It wasn’t always that way, and I would argue as Christians we need to recover the prominence and importance of the rainbow for what it was originally meant to be. In this article, we will explore the Noahic Covenant as a foundational principle within the Christian faith since it is the original Rainbow covenant and the only one worth mentioning here today. The Noahic Covenant holds a significant place within the grand narrative of Scripture, representing a pivotal moment in the unfolding plan of God's redemption for humanity.

The aftermath of the Great Flood as it is recorded in the book of Genesis serves as the foundation for the Noahic Covenant. God, in accordance with His justice, decided to use the floodwaters to purge the earth when humans slid into widespread depravity. But because of his grace, God looked favorably upon Noah, and he was given the order to construct an ark to protect himself, his family, and a representative of every living thing.

God established the Noahic Covenant with Noah and all succeeding generations after the flood waters subsided. Genesis 9:8–17 contains a description of this covenant. It is crucial to realize that God's covenantal faithfulness is extended by the Noahic Covenant, which provides a continuation of His redemptive mission for humanity. This is not a stand-alone covenant. It follows in succession with the Covenant of Grace.

Numerous promises made in the Noahic Covenant have profound religious importance. God pledges to never again flood the planet, first and foremost. As a symbol of this covenant, He creates a rainbow to serve as a reminder of His loyalty to His word and His enduring benevolence.

The sanctity of human life is likewise emphasized by this covenant. God forbids humanity from shedding human blood, stressing the intrinsic worth and dignity of those who bear His image. As humanity is given the duty of ruling over the earth and its creatures, the Noahic Covenant strengthens the idea of human stewardship.

Most importantly, it continues to emphasize the idea of blessing and prosperity for those who keep the covenant, as well as affirming the curses and punishments awaiting those who choose to disobey. This could very easily go into a whole discussion on covenant theology, but I’ll let you read my mediocre blog on the matter instead.

The Noahic Covenant plays a significant role in the grand story of the Bible, connecting with the larger themes of creation, sin, atonement, and restoration. It connects the events of the pre-flood world with the developing scheme of God's atonement through Christ. The redemptive pattern found throughout Scripture is echoed in the Noahic Covenant. Foreshadowing the ultimate salvation that would come via Jesus Christ, God provided a means of salvation and protection for Noah and his family. The salvation provided by Christ's death on the cross is the final fulfillment of God's love and faithfulness in saving a righteous remnant in the ark.

The Noahic Covenant also serves as a foundation for God's other established covenants, including the Abrahamic, Mosaic, and eventually the New Covenant in Christ. Each new covenant builds on the one before it, culminating in the redemptive act of Jesus Christ. Again, this ties into the broader discussion on the beauty of covenant theology.

The Noahic Covenant is a testament to God's faithfulness, mercy, and sovereign plan of salvation because of its biblical-theological roots and place in the larger biblical story. It serves as a link between the pre-flood world and God's developing salvation plan, marking a crucial point in human history.

As we contemplate the Noahic Covenant, we are reminded of God's steadfast love, His commitment to His creation, and His ultimate desire to reconcile humanity to Himself. This covenant points forward to the redemptive work of Jesus Christ, who offers salvation, not just from physical destruction, but from the power and consequences of sin.

May the Noahic Covenant serve as a reminder of God's faithfulness throughout generations, inspiring us to live in faithful obedience, stewardship, and anticipation of the fulfillment of all His promises in Christ.

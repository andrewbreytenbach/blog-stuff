The Majestic Glory of Christ's Resurrection


The resurrection of Jesus Christ is an event of unrivaled significance in the archives of human history, embodying both the apex of divine love and the fulfillment of God's eternal plan. The resurrection not only signified Christ's triumph over the grave, but also the ultimate vindication of His life and ministry. It is the stamp of approval, the highest affirmation, of each and every doctrine Jesus ever taught. It is in this grand narrative of redemption that we can perceive the exquisite beauty of the resurrection – a beauty that transcends our limited human understanding, and imprints the hearts of believers with a profound sense of wonder and awe.

At the core of Reformed theology is the idea that the glory of God is most profoundly displayed in the person and work of Jesus Christ. In the resurrection, we see the glorious culmination of Christ's earthly ministry, as well as the beginning of a new era in God's redemptive plan, the church age. This is the most significant event in the history of the world, make no mistake. It logically flows, then, that there are numerous implications for how Christians ought to live their lives then.

The practical implications of the resurrection are both profound and far-reaching. The resurrection provides the believer with an unshakeable hope – a hope rooted in the knowledge that Christ has conquered death, and that through Him, we too shall overcome the grave. This hope, born of the glorious reality of the resurrection, serves as an anchor for our souls, enabling us to withstand the storms of life and to navigate the treacherous waters of a fallen world.

I would also be remiss if I did not stress the importance of the resurrection as the basis for our sanctification. As believers, we are called to be conformed to the image of Christ, and it is in the resurrection that we find the ultimate pattern for godly living. Through the power of the risen Christ, we are enabled to put to death the deeds of the flesh, and to walk in the newness of life, a resurrected life. In this sense, the resurrection not only holds out the promise of future glory, but also empowers us to live lives of holiness and devotion in the present.

In contemplating the beauty and the glory of the resurrection, we must not neglect the significance of Christ's ongoing intercessory work on our behalf. It is through the resurrection that Christ has been exalted to the right hand of the Father, where He continues to intercede for us as our great High Priest. This truth, which lies at the very heart of the believer's assurance, serves as a constant reminder of the intimate and enduring relationship that exists between Christ and His people.

The beauty and the glory of the resurrection of Jesus Christ is a theme of inexhaustible depth and richness. It is a doctrine that encompasses not only the triumphant victory of Christ over death, but also the manifold practical implications that flow from this momentous event. As believers, we are called to meditate upon the resurrection, and to allow its transformative power to shape our lives and mold our hearts. In doing so, we will find ourselves drawn ever deeper into the mystery and majesty of Christ's love, and our souls will yearn for his return, where one day we'll be united with Christ for all eternity.

~ An Unworthy Servant

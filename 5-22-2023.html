A Review of "Prayer: How Praying Together Shapes the Church" by John Onwuchekwa


The book "Prayer: How Praying Together Shapes the Church" by John Onwuchekwa is a rallying cry for Christians to rediscover the importance and power of corporate prayer. Onwuchekwa elevates the significance of prayer in a brief but profound analysis of this spiritual exercise, reminding us that it is not just a private or personal activity but also a crucial practice that forms the identity, mission, and unity of the Church.

The book's central thesis is clearly stated: prayer is the Church's heartbeat and a lifeline to God that binds His people as a whole. The author believes that prayer should be a collaborative effort and that it should focus more on aligning ourselves with God's will than on changing our circumstances. This viewpoint forces us to reevaluate how we view prayer and our place in the Church in a society that is becoming more and more individualistic.

Acts 2:42's description of the early Christian Church's devotion to prayer serves as the framework for Onwuchekwa's narrative. The early Christians' oneness revealed a shared devotion to prayer that had a significant impact on their community and brought about growth and transformation. He then exhorts the Church of today to rediscover this same devotion.

The book is divided into three key sections that build on one another. Onwuchekwa discusses the theology of prayer in the first section, outlining its benefits. He makes the claim that prayer is not just a spiritual practice, but also a way for God to carry out His purposes.

The author explores the communal aspect of prayer in the second section. He elaborates on how group prayer fortifies the Church by promoting harmony, humility, and reliance on God. He persuasively makes the case that when we pray together, we are reminded of our shared need for God's grace, which strengthens the Church.

A strong call to action is made in the closing portion. In his writing, Onwuchekwa exhorts churches to make corporate prayer a top priority throughout their gatheringsâ€”not as a ritualistic extra, but as a crucial element of worship. This book is pastorally applicable in addition to being theologically enlightening since the author offers helpful guidance on how to manage a church that is prayer-filled and deals with potential difficulties.

This book genuinely shines in Onwuchekwa's emphasis on the group aspect of prayer. He criticizes the Church's frequently individualistic approach to this spiritual practice by advocating prayer as a social activity rather than a solitary one. His belief that group prayer may mold and fortify the Church rings loud and clear throughout the short book.

For Christians who want to better understand prayer and its function in the Church, "Prayer: How Praying Together Shapes the Church" is a necessary read. In addition to calling for prayer, Onwuchekwa's book also promotes unity, humility, and a reliance on God that underpins every facet of our church communities.

This book serves as an effective remedy at a time when individualism and fragmentation may find their way deep into our churches by reminding us of the crucial role that prayer plays in forming a biblical community. It upends the status quo by encouraging us to not just pray but to pray together, to stand as the body of Christ in unity, and to mold the Church into becoming more like Christ by our common love to God.

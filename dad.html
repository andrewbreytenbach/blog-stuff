A Child's Glory


As we journey through life, it is important to recognize the value of familial relationships and the blessings that come from them. Modern culture will reject that statement. It will tell you to push back and embrace who you are, leaving your family and other values behind in the wake. Yet in Proverbs 17:6, we encounter a powerful statement about the connections between a family:

"Grandchildren are the crown of the aged, and the glory of children is their fathers." (ESV)

This verse offers us profound insights into the beauty and significance of family ties, as well as the importance of honoring our elders and understanding our roles within the family unit. In this blog post, I will explore the meaning behind this verse, highlighting the key points that can guide our daily lives.

Grandchildren as the Crown of the Aged

The first part of the verse speaks of grandchildren as the crown of the aged. The imagery of a crown brings to mind ideas of honor, authority, and respect. In ancient cultures, a crown was worn by those of high rank and symbolized the pinnacle of achievement. Here, the crown represents the joy, fulfillment, and reward that grandparents experience in their later years as they witness the growth and success of their grandchildren.

Grandparents have a unique role in the lives of their grandchildren. They often provide love, support, and wisdom that can guide the younger generation through life's challenges. The bond between grandparent and grandchild can be a source of great joy and satisfaction for both parties, fostering a deep sense of connection and purpose.

The Glory of Children is Their Fathers

The second part of the verse emphasizes the relationship between children and their fathers. It states that the glory of children is their fathers, which suggests that children find their pride and sense of worth in their relationship with their fathers. This implies a responsibility on the part of fathers to provide their children with guidance, love, and support.

In this context, "glory" can be understood as a reflection of the father's character, values, and virtues. As children grow up, they often seek to emulate their fathers, adopting their beliefs, principles, and behaviors. This highlights the immense responsibility and influence fathers have in shaping the lives of their children.

This isn’t just a biblical teaching. The famous song “Cat’s in the Cradle” was a regular tune in my house growing up, and my father would always remind me after the song ended that the message rang true– children tend to grow up reflecting their characters and values.

For my father, who will read this later, all I can say is thank you. Thank you for giving me and my brother an excellent glory to behold. Thank you for raising us in the fear of the Lord, teaching us obedience, wisdom, love, respect, and hard work, all the values we find in the book of Proverbs. It is an immense responsibility to be a father, and I have no doubt you left an eternal mark on my brother and I for our good. Thank you.

The Importance of Intergenerational Relationships in an Individualistic Culture

Proverbs 17:6 highlights the significance of strong intergenerational relationships within families, despite what Western culture seems to prioritize. The connections between grandparents, parents, and children are essential for the health and well-being of all involved. These relationships provide opportunities for the transfer of wisdom, values, and traditions from one generation to the next. The family unit is stronger together, not apart.

Furthermore, strong family bonds contribute to a sense of belonging and identity for individuals, fostering resilience and stability in times of change or adversity. By nurturing these relationships, we invest in the strength and continuity of our families, communities, and societies.

Conclusion

What does this mean for you? Well, if you have a father who left you an amazing glory to treasure, thank them. That’s all you need to do. If you’re a parent or grandparent, consider the impact and legacy your decisions will leave behind. It is no small task to be a parent, but by walking in the fear of the Lord and in submission to the Scriptures, we are able to raise up the next generation of warriors for Christ.

~ An Unworthy Servant

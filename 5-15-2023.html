A Gut-Check: “The Life and Diary of David Brainerd” by Jonathan Edwards


This week’s book review and recommendation covers the most challenging book I read so far this year: “The Life and Diary of David Brainerd”. In “The Life and Diary of David Brainerd,” Jonathan Edwards has encapsulated the fervent, relentless, and self-sacrificing life of an 18th-century missionary whose influence extends far beyond his temporary existence. The book, a compilation of David Brainerd's journal entries and Edwards' own reflections, provides a piercing glance into Brainerd's spiritual journey, marked by moments of deep despair, profound hope, and unyielding faith.

At the heart of Brainerd's life was an unwavering commitment to the gospel and an intense yearning to bring it to the Native American population. This was a man who battled not only external hardships such as sickness, solitude, and harsh weather conditions, but also internal struggles of self-doubt, melancholy, and spiritual torment. However, it is in this wrestling with tangible and intangible adversity that we discover the central theme of the book: the transformative power of unwavering faith in the face of life's relentless trials.

The depth of Brainerd's inner turmoil is mirrored only by his deep devotion to God. His diary entries are filled with a refreshing honesty that shatters the stereotype of the "perfect" missionary, illuminating instead a man who is deeply human, struggling with the same doubts and fears that beset us all. The difference, however, lies in Brainerd's relentless pursuit of God, a pursuit that was not deterred by his own weaknesses or the stark reality of his circumstances. It was fueled by relying on the grace found only in the gospel of Jesus Christ.

Edwards expertly interweaves his analysis and commentary with Brainerd's diaries, offering readers an intimate portrayal of Brainerd's spiritual journey. He navigates the complex dichotomy of Brainerd's life — a man torn between the throes of depression and the ecstasy of divine communion — with an empathetic understanding that only a fellow believer could muster.

Why is this book essential for Christians today? Firstly, it serves as a potent reminder of the transformative power of faith. The narrative is a testament to how one's personal faith can drive not just individual transformation but also catalyze a profound impact on communities. Brainnerd’s faith was a zealous, white-hot flame that burned with passion for the glory of God and the advancement of the gospel.

Secondly, Brainerd's life story prompts us to reconsider the paradigm of Christian success. In a time when success is often measured by numbers — be it followers, likes, or church attendees — Brainerd's story reveals that the real impact lies in the depth of one's faith and the authenticity of one's ministry.

Thirdly, the book underscores the importance of resilience in faith. Brainerd's life was a continual struggle, a battle fought both within and without. Yet, he remained steadfast in his devotion, a testament to the enduring power of faith in God, even amidst adversity.

Lastly, the book challenges the modern Christian to reflect on the depth of their commitment to Christ. Brainerd's raw and intimate reflections on his struggles and victories provoke us to question the authenticity of our faith and our willingness to sacrifice for the Gospel.

In conclusion, 'The Life and Diary of David Brainerd' is not just a biography; it is a mirror held up to the modern Christian. It calls us to reflect, to question, and to examine our faith in light of Brainerd's undying commitment to God. The book is a poignant reminder of the transformative power of faith and an enduring testament to the impact one committed individual can have. It is a must-read for anyone who yearns for a deeper, more authentic faith.

~ An Unworthy Servant

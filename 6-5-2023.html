The Main Themes of the First Epistle of John


The First Epistle of John, which is part of the New Testament, offers us profound theological and ethical insights from the viewpoint of an apostle who was completely engrossed in the meaning and intent of the message of Christ. I'm going to expand on these main ideas, paying particular attention to the fundamental ideas of communion with God, the duality of light and darkness, the irrefutable criteria for authenticity, and the profound love God has for His children, as they are given in this beautiful text.

One of the most important themes—fellowship with God—is brought up in the epistle's start. In his letter to the church, the apostle John states, "that which we have seen and heard we proclaim also to you, so that you too may have fellowship with us; and indeed our fellowship is with the Father and with his Son Jesus Christ." (1 John 1:3). Our connection with the Father, who is the Sovereign God, is fashioned by our faith in His Son, Jesus Christ. This relationship involves our continual commitment to live by God's commandments and to imitate His holy nature, a principle emphasized throughout Reformed theology. It is not only relational.

The idea of light and darkness is closely related to communion with God. This contrast is strongly presented in the first chapter, which declares that "God is light, and there is no darkness at all in Him" (1 John 1:5). As Christians, we have a responsibility to avoid the darkness of sin and deception and instead walk in the light, which is the divine standard for moral excellence and truth. This dichotomous metaphor urges Christians to continuously match their lives with the righteousness embodied in God's light, acting as an ethical compass.

The Epistle also offers a critical examination of authenticity by calling into question the legitimacy of our professed beliefs. According to the apostle John, "Whoever says, 'I know him,' but does not do what he commands is a liar, and the truth is not in that person" (1 John 2:4). This topic supports the idea that faith is shown in acts and serves as a reminder to believers that adherence to God's commands is the outward expression of our true connection with Him. It reiterates the doctrine of justification by grace alone via faith alone (sola fide), but it also recognizes that genuine faith always results in good deeds.

Last but not least, the Epistle is infused with the idea of love—love for God and love from God. "See what great love the Father has lavished on us, that we should be called children of God!" the apostle John exclaims in a stirring revelation. We are exactly that. (1 John 3:1). This abundant and unconditional love of God grounds our faith and inspires us to love God and others in return. It encourages us to uphold the Great Commandment, which is to love God with all of our hearts and our neighbors as ourselves. This is a fundamental Christian calling.

These four themes, which are intertwined throughout 1 John, show the apostle's intention to lead believers toward a sincere, loving, and duty-bound relationship with God. They act as potent reminders of God's limitless love, the sacredness of His laws, and the transformative power of living in His light. It is my sincere prayer that these insights would inspire people to recommit themselves to the road of righteousness, in accordance with the basic ideas of the gospel and the eternal foundation of Christian faith.

~ An Unworthy Servant

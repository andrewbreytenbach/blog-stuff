God's Sequence


In our journey to uncover the intricate and beautiful truths of the universe, we often stumble upon hidden patterns and designs that provoke deep thought and reflection. One such pattern that has captivated scientists, mathematicians, and theologians alike is the Fibonacci sequence. Many believe that its discovery by Leonardo of Pisa, also known as Fibonacci, was not a mere accident, but rather an intentional revelation of the grand design of creation by a divine hand. In this blog post, I will delve into the theological idea that all truth is God's truth and explore how Fibonacci's sequence is interwoven into the fabric of nature, providing a profound testament to the natural revelation espoused in Psalm 19.

As a renowned mathematician, Fibonacci is well-known for his contributions to the world of mathematics. His eponymous sequence, which begins with 0 and 1 and continues with each subsequent number being the sum of the two preceding ones (e.g., 0, 1, 1, 2, 3, 5, 8, 13, 21, and so on), can be found in various aspects of the natural world. This pervasive presence of the Fibonacci sequence points to a grand design, one that is often attributed to the divine, and rightly so. It is not merely coincidence that the ‘golden ratio’ is prevalent almost everywhere we turn in the created world.

Psalm 19:1-4 declares, "The heavens tell of the glory of God; and their expanse declares the work of His hands. Day to day pours forth speech, and night to night reveals knowledge. There is no speech, nor are there words; their voice is not heard. Their line has gone out into all the earth, and their words to the end of the world." It is in this context that we can appreciate the Fibonacci sequence as a means of natural revelation, a testimony to God's handiwork. Nature is testifying and shouting to the world the truth that there is a sovereign Creator who exists, a master craftsman who made all things.

Take, for example, the arrangement of seeds in the head of a sunflower. As they radiate out from the center, the seeds form spirals that adhere to the Fibonacci sequence, allowing for maximum seed density and optimal exposure to sunlight. Similarly, the spiral patterns in pinecones, pineapples, and many other plants reveal the same Fibonacci proportions.

In the animal kingdom, we find the Fibonacci sequence in the growth patterns of various species. The chambered nautilus, a marine mollusk, forms its shell by adding progressively larger chambers as it grows, with each chamber following the Fibonacci sequence in size. Even the breeding patterns of rabbits, as observed by Fibonacci himself, can be represented by his famous sequence. And there are numerous more examples I could point to, like the spiral of a galaxy or the spin of a hurricane as instances in nature that adhere to this principle.

Moreover, the golden ratio (approximately 1.618), derived from the Fibonacci sequence, has been observed in the proportions of various objects in the natural world, from the dimensions of the Great Pyramid of Giza to the structure of the human body. This ubiquitous presence of the golden ratio points to a divine architect, one who has imprinted His signature upon creation.

Fibonacci's discovery of the sequence that bears his name is a testament to the theological concept that all truth is God's truth, and that Fibonacci’s sequence is not Fibonacci’s at all, but God’s sequence. By studying the natural world and uncovering its hidden patterns, we not only gain insight into the workings of the universe but also catch a glimpse of the divine hand that brought everything into existence. The Fibonacci sequence serves as a powerful reminder of the words of Psalm 19, reinforcing the idea that the natural world is a conduit for God's revelation and an expression of His glory. As we continue to explore the mysteries of the universe, let us see the transcendent truths that lie hidden within the fabric of creation, waiting to be discovered causing us to worship our Sovereign King.

~ An Unworthy Servant

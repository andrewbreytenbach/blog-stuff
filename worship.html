A Word on Words


Yesterday, I was sitting in church on the Lord’s Day, worshiping and being joyful, when I thought about why we choose to sing the songs we sing in church. What is the reason or a methodology behind it?

As Christians, we understand the essential nature of theology in every aspect of our faith. From the way we read and interpret Scripture, to our understanding of God's sovereignty and grace, theology forms the foundation of our beliefs. One crucial area where theology is to be applied is in the worship of our Lord, particularly in the lyrics we sing.

The act of worship is not merely an emotional response to God's presence, but a time when we come together as a body of believers to proclaim the truth of God's Word and to honor Him with our hearts and minds. Worship must be grounded in theological knowledge, and the lyrics we sing should be a reflection of this.

Theological Knowledge Honors God

At the core of the Bible’s teaching on worship is a deep reverence for God's sovereignty and holiness. The lyrics we sing should reflect this high view of God, ensuring that the truths we proclaim align with Scripture. When we sing songs that are theologically accurate, we are honoring God by acknowledging His greatness, His character, and His work of redemption in our lives.

Theological Knowledge Unifies Believers

Worship is a time for the body of Christ to come together in unity, proclaiming our shared faith in the one true God. When we sing songs with theologically rich lyrics, we are strengthening the bonds of unity in our congregation. These lyrics allow us to focus on the essentials of our faith, avoiding division caused by shallow or inaccurate theology. Yes, the saying is true, doctrine divides. But, the corollary must also be true: nothing unites like doctrine. It's why creeds and confessions are so powerful!

Theological Knowledge Deepens Our Understanding of Scripture

As we sing songs with deep theological content, we are reminded of biblical truths that we might otherwise overlook. These songs can serve as powerful tools for teaching, helping to embed the doctrines of our faith in our hearts and minds. The more we sing and internalize these lyrics, the greater our understanding of Scripture becomes, leading to a deeper relationship with our Savior.

Theological Knowledge Fosters Spiritual Growth

When we engage in worship that is saturated with theological knowledge, we are nurturing our spiritual growth. As we meditate on the profound truths found in the lyrics of these songs, our hearts are moved to a deeper love for God, and our faith is strengthened. Singing theologically rich lyrics can also spur us to action, inspiring us to live out our faith in practical ways and to be diligent in our pursuit of holiness.

Theological Knowledge Guards Against False Teaching

In a world where false teaching and doctrinal error abound, it is vital that we hold fast to the truth of God's Word. By emphasizing theological knowledge in our worship, we are equipping believers to discern truth from error. When we sing lyrics grounded in biblical theology, we are building a strong foundation that can withstand the challenges of our culture and the temptations of false doctrine.

The importance of theologically rich lyrics in worship cannot be overstated. As believers, we must ensure that our worship is grounded in the knowledge of God's Word and the truth of the Gospel. By singing songs that honor God with their lyrics, we prepare ourselves for what we will be doing for all of eternity: worshiping God in Spirit and in truth. I hope this unusually short blog has been both edifying and insightful for you as you begin the week.

~ An Unworthy Servant
